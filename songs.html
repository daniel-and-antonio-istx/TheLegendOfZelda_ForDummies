Zelda's Lullaby

Used in Ocarina of Time. Zelda’s Lullaby is taught to you by Impa, and it is used in front of Triforce symbols throughout the game to do various things.

Saria's Song

Used in Ocarina of Time. This song’s main purpose is to allow Link to speak to Saria through Navi, though it is used once to lift the spirits of Darunia, leader of the Gorons.

Sun's Song

Used in Ocarina of Time. Learned beneath a grave, the Sun’s Song is not a compulsory song for completing the game. Its main purpose is for fast-forwarding time to morning or evening, and it is also used for freezing ReDeads and Gibdos.

Minuet of Forest

Used in Ocarina of Time. Teleports you to the Forest Temple in the Sacred Forest Meadow.

Bolero of Fire

Used in Ocarina of Time. Teleports you to the Fire Temple in the Death Mountain Crater.

Serenade of Water

Used in Ocarina of Time. Teleports you to the Water Temple in Lake Hylia.

Nocturne of Shadow

Used in Ocarina of Time. Teleports you to the Water Temple in Lake Hylia.

Requiem of Spirit

Used in Ocarina of Time. Teleports you to the Spirit Temple in the Desert Colossus.

Song of Healing

Used in Majora’s Mask. This song is taught to you by the Happy Mask Salesman, and is primarily used to turn restless souls into wearable masks with different powers.

Song of Soaring

Used in Majora’s Mask. Taught to you by an owl statue, this song allows for travel to any activated Owl Statue throughout the land of Termina.

Inverted Song of Time

 Used in Majora’s Mask. Essentially just the Song of Time, but played in reverse. The song slows down the flow of time.

Goron Lullaby

Used in Majora’s Mask. A freezing old Goron initially teaches you part of this song, but Goron Link learns the rest of it later on. The song is the opposite of the Sonata of Awakening - it puts the Baby Goron to sleep (as well as all the other Gorons). It is then used to put the giant Goron blocking access to the Snowhead Temple to sleep.

Sonata of Awakening

Used in Majora’s Mask. Only used a select few times, it is taught to Deku Link by a trapped monkey who had supposedly captured the Deku Princess. It is shown as a loud song that can wake anybody up from a deep slumber.

 

Song of Double Time

Used in Majora’s Mask. Essentially just the first three notes of the Song of Time but played twice each. In the original N64 version, this song skipped time to the next morning or evening, but in the 3DS remake, the song instead skipped time to wherever the player wished, so long as it was after the current time.

New Wave Bossa Nova

Used in Majora’s Mask. Taught to you by a Zora named Mikau before his death, the New Wave Bossa Nova only has one use, which is to recover the voice of another Zora named Lulu so she can awaken a giant turtle and allow access to the Great Bay Temple.

Elegy of Emptiness

Used in Majora’s Mask. The Elegy of Emptiness, when played, produces a statue (or “shell”) of your current form, which can be used to push buttons and solve puzzles (except for Deku Link’s statue, which is too light for any of that).

Oath to Order

Used in Majora’s Mask. Oath to Order is a song taught to you by the Four Giants after clearing the Woodfall Temple, and is used for summoning them to hold the Moon in place after collecting the four Remains of the temple guardians.

Epona's Song

 Used in Ocarina of Time and Majora’s Mask. In both games, the song is used to allow Link to summon his horse, Epona. It can only be used when Link is an adult in Ocarina of Time, and there are some areas in both games where the song has no effect.

Song of Time

Used in Ocarina of Time and Majora’s Mask. In Ocarina of Time, the song is used to open the Door of Time, allowing for access to the Master Sword’s pedestal, as well as for activating Time Blocks in other locations. In Majora’s Mask, the song is used to rewind time to the beginning of the three-day cycle.

Song of Storms

Used in Ocarina of Time and Majora’s Mask. The Song of Storms is used for creating a storm, no matter where it’s played. Sometimes it can reveal fairies, but its main purpose is for opening progress to later parts of the games.
